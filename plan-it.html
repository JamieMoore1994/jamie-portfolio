Live Music In Manchester

About this project

Plan It was another student project in the second year of University at The Manchester Metropolitan University – but the title of the unit for this project being Digital Project Management. Plan It is a website that showcases places people can go and things people can do within the Manchester City Centre – similar to the Live Music in Manchester website – but this project was more of a focus on project management and how we manage the project instead of the quality of the code and website.

The team were tasked with producing a prototype digital product of our choice and after a discussion the Plan It website was our idea. There was a team of seven of us involved in this project, it had its ups and downs, but we worked together efficiently and achieved a mark of 70%.

My role in this project

Similar to the live music project, the team had to pick roles they would like to take in this project, and I put myself forward for the project management role. I do this because I want to be outside my comfort zone and put in situations that test my abilities to organise and execute a project. It was challenging managing this project because like every student project, there can be times when students are not completing the tasks allocated to them and you have to be constantly asking for updates – especially with a deadline looming.

The Digital Project Management had a mixture of students on a web development degree and digital marketing degree, so our team had a mixture of skillset. I took on the role to code this website as the rest of my team were on the digital marketing degree and not comfortable coding this website. Luckily, I had already taught myself some basic flexbox and css grid from my experience in the Live Music project, so I felt a lot more comfortable coding this website up.

What went well in this project

The start of this project was very challenging and un-organised, so the team had to come together towards the end of the project to work even harder. I give a lot of credit to the team; they did excellent to pull together and produce good standard work.

Communication was a vital factor to producing a successful project and towards the later phase of the project, the team were great with this. Every team member was airing out their problems with their delegated tasks and asking for feedback on their work, whereas in the beginning of the project this was not happening

We had a mixed range of skills in the group, so it was easier to go capture content such as the images and the creative video one team member produced for the website. The whole team shared the common goal of wanting to do well on this project to achieve a good mark and looking back at this project, we achieved our goal.

My thoughts on what did not go well on this project

The research phase was to a poor standard in my opinion for this project, as we did not gather enough user feedback and opinions. The team could have been more proactive in this phase and got more people involved with the research to gather more feedback on live music events they would like to see and places they would like to eat – as this was a feature on the website. There could have been a more considered information architecture on the website too if we had got more users involved in the open card sorting and tree jacking exercises.

My only other critique is that I wish I had started learning flexbox and CSS grid way earlier. Although I gathered more knowledge during the build process, looking back the HTML and CSS files, I could have cut out a lot of lines of pointless code. Also, I could have structured the CSS file more efficiently to make it more readable and maintainable in the future.
